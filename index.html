How does this happen?

The Myth of Meritocracy.

Myth:

The most qualified candidate gets the job.

Truth:

Some qualified people never make it into consideration because of unconscious bias.

I am giving folks the benefit of the doubt, that the bias is unconscious. I'm sure there is conscious bias, too. But I believe most folks would want to be inclusive if given the chance.

Racial Bias in Hiring

A November 2002 study by the University of Chicago Graduate School of Business

From July 2001 to May 2002, 2 professors sent fictitious resumes in response to 1,300 help-wanted ads listed in the Boston Globe and the Chicago Tribune. They used the callback rate for interviews to measure the success of each resume.

Applicants with "white-sounding" names were 50 percent more likely to get called for an interview than applicants with "African-American-sounding" names.

The most surprising and disheartening result is seeing that applicants with African-American names were not rewarded for having better resumes.

Marianne Bertrand "Racial Bias in Hiring."
University of Chicago Graduate School of Business, November 2002

White applicants with higher-quality resumes received 30% more callbacks than those with lower-quality resumes. Having a higher-quality resume has a much smaller impact on African-American applicants. They only experienced 9% more callbacks for the same resume improvement.

Kim O'Grady

I made one change that day. I put Mr in front of my name on my CV. ...I got an interview for the very next job I applied for. And the one after that.

Science faculty’s subtle gender biases favor male students

A November 2012 study by Yale University

In a randomized double-blind study, 127 STEM professors from across the country were asked to evaluate the resumes of a student. All resumes were identical, except half had the name "John" and the other half the name "Jennifer".
Faculty participants rated the male applicant as significantly more competent and hire-able than the (identical) female applicant.

Corinne A. Moss-Racusin, John F. Dovidio, Victoria L. Brescoll, Mark J. Graham, and Jo Handelsman "Science faculty’s subtle gender biases favor male students."
Yale University, November 2012

The scientists in the study were less willing to mentor Jennifer or to hire her as a lab manager. They also recommended paying her a lower salary. Jennifer was offered, on average, $4,000 per year (13%) less than John.

Illegal Interview Questions

That was an example something that NEVER should be said to someone during an interview, but let's look at questions you shouldn't and legally can't ask at interviews.
How old are you?
The Age Discrimination in Employment Act of 1967 forbids employment discrimination against anyone at least 40 years of age in the United States.
What is your race, color or ethnicity?
Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 is a federal law that prohibits employers from discriminating against employees on the basis of sex, race, color, national origin, and religion.
Do you have children or plan to?
Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 is a federal law that prohibits employers from discriminating against employees on the basis of sex (and pregnancy status), race, color, national origin, and religion.
What is your religious affiliation?

Are you disabled?

Let's make it better!

Listen.

AKA Shut up and listen. When someone from a marginalized group you are not a part of shares a story of experiencing bias, it's a gift. It's a chance to understand things from another person's point of view. Resist the urge to follow up with "oh, that happened to me once" or "that doesn't happen." Just listen mindfully and reply with "that sucks, I'm sorry that happened."

Think.

Now that you know unconscious bias is a real thing that has been researched and proven, question why you're putting a resume in the reject pile. Strive for a true Meritocracy.

Realize diversity is an asset.

We've seen how diversity brings value to your company - It makes your team better problem solvers and more understanding of your end-users. This is a benefit, this brings overall greater value to your business than just a set of skills. To quote my friend Marc, a fellow queer web agency owner...
